# CCMR On Track Weekly — One-Pager Guide

*A rapid orientation so you can open the workbook and start using it with confidence.*

---

## What it is

CCMR On Track Weekly is a **weekly, campus-specific Excel workbook** that shows where your seniors (and other grades, where available) stand on College, Career, and Military Readiness — and helps you decide who needs intervention this week.

It's built to run a simple weekly rhythm: **orient → diagnose → act → revisit.**

## What it is *not*

- It is **not** a replacement for your SIS, gradebook, or transcript system.
- It is **not** a web app or dashboard — it's a spreadsheet you filter and sort.
- It does **not** push data back upstream or fix district data pipelines.
- It does **not** require a Salesforce license or BI tooling.

---

## Where to find it each week

You'll receive a fresh workbook on your campus's configured schedule, delivered to your district's agreed location (e.g., Google Drive, Box, or a shared folder — confirmed during onboarding). When it arrives:

1. **Save it** to your team's shared location.
2. **Confirm the campus and week** using the identifying info on the **Menu** tab (e.g., "Lake Travis HS — 3/31/2026").
3. **Note the data cutoff** — the "Updated" dates on the Menu tab tell you how current each data source is.

> **Confidential data.** The workbook carries protected student information. It is for campus leadership, advisors, and counselors only — do not share it outside authorized users. For any questions, submit a ticket through the support portal (the **Get Help** tab).

## How to navigate (Release 1 tabs)

| Tab | What it answers |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Menu** | Where am I? What's in this workbook, and how current is each source? |
| **Summary** | Where does the campus stand this week — enrollment, TSI, pipeline, EOC, subgroups? |
| **All Data** | Who exactly? The full student list — filter and sort to build any working list. |
| **TSI By Assessment** | How are students meeting TSI (by pathway, GPA band, course grade), and who's close? |
| **Counselors** | How does each counselor's caseload look on TSI and the college pipeline? |
| **Advisors** | Same view, organized by advisor. |
| **Grade Levels** | Is the readiness gap a senior issue or earlier (by "Class of" cohort)? |

*A hidden `rawdata` tab feeds every view — you won't interact with it directly.*

---

## Top 5 filters (start on the All Data tab)

1. **TSI Math not met** → candidates for Texas College Bridge, tutoring, or a retake.
2. **Strong GPA band but not TSI Met** → "low-hanging fruit" for a quick win.
3. **By counselor or advisor** → build a caseload-specific list to hand off.
4. **Close to a threshold** (e.g., near the TSI cut score, or close to 15 SCH in dual credit) → one nudge from done.
5. **Pipeline gaps** (no application, FAFSA not submitted, Path Form not done) → outreach list.

> Tip: filtered lists copy/paste cleanly into an email or a separate tracker so the right staff can act immediately.

---

## Top 5 questions to answer this week

1. **What changed** since last week — progress, regressions, new students off-track?
2. **Where are we stuck** — which group or pathway is lagging on the Summary tab?
3. **Which names** show up on the priority list?
4. **What intervention** applies to which students?
5. **Who owns** each next step, and by when?

---

## Recommended weekly meeting agenda (15–30 min)

1. **Orient (3 min):** Open Menu, confirm week, glance at Summary for what moved.
2. **Pick a topic (1 min):** TSI, a caseload, a pipeline step — whatever is most urgent.
3. **Diagnose (5–10 min):** Use the relevant tab to find the priority students.
4. **Decide (5–10 min):** Match interventions to students.
5. **Assign (3–5 min):** Name an owner and a next step for each student/group.
6. **Set the revisit:** Plan to reopen the same tab next week to check movement.

*Match attendees to the topic — e.g., testing coordinator and CCR lead for TSI; dual credit coordinator for dual credit; advising/outreach for the pipeline.*

## A few things to know about the numbers

- **Counts, not percentages.** Metrics show absolute numbers and "N of total" (e.g., "186 of 407"), because percentage targets don't work well for campuses already above benchmark.
- **TSI Met is calculated in the workbook** from the highest qualifying assessment score (TSIA2, SAT, or ACT) against state cut scores. A "Met by" label tells you which test did it.
- **Charts update when you filter** within a tab — but filters stay inside that tab and don't change the Summary.
- **Colors:** navy = on-track, amber = not met / off-track. (Red/green is avoided for accessibility.)
- **Hidden columns** mean that data either isn't available or isn't reliable for your campus yet — by design, so you don't act on bad data.

---

## What to do if something looks wrong

- **A number changed week to week?** That's expected — the data refreshes weekly (see the FAQ for why).
- **A student record looks incorrect?** Don't act on it. Submit a ticket through the support portal (the **Get Help** tab) — corrections happen upstream in the source systems.
- **A whole column is blank or missing?** It's likely hidden because the data isn't trustworthy yet. Flag it through the support portal so it can be added when ready.
